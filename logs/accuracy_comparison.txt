  Key takeaways:

  The raise improvements are the headline. Raise-or-not went from 76.6% → 90.5% — the oversampling worked. Exact raise class hit 83.1% and within-1-class is 88.2%, so when the model is off it's usually just one bucket away (e.g. 2bb vs 2.5bb).

  The postflop gains (+10-12pp on flop/turn, +15pp on river) are mostly a metric change — the old model had to match exact dollar amounts for raises, which was nearly impossible. The discretized classes make this achievable. That said, the
  underlying decision quality genuinely improved too, as shown by raise-or-not going up substantially.

  One regression: call dropped from 87.1% (texture) to 82.3%. Worth watching but small compared to the raise gains.

  One caveat on the total comparison: the 84.4% → 89.1% headline overstates the "real" quality gain because the old "exact" was punishing the model for raise sizing errors that no longer matter under class matching. The raise-or-not number (76.6% →
   90.5%) is the cleanest apples-to-apples signal.
